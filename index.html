The Cooking Skill: 1–99 at home, 100–120 in the wild

Culinary school restructured as a RuneScape skill. Fifteen tiers from never-boiled-an-egg to leading a kitchen worth a special journey — each unlocks techniques, sets quests with pass conditions, and ends with a boss that gates the next tier.

The game engine. XP runs on the actual RuneScape curve, tuned so finishing a tier lands you exactly at the top of its level band. Skills are worth 1×, quests 3×, bosses 6×. Drills require reps: a quest with pips (● ● ○) must be landed on separate days — click to log each rep; a skill is mastered on the third success, not the first. Eight sub-disciplines level independently in the stats panel below. Progress saves in this browser.

The cape and the true cap. Level 99 (13,034,431 xp) earns the skillcape: complete home mastery, the honest ceiling of self-teaching. But the real cap is 120 (104,273,167 xp — yes, the curve keeps going, and yes, 100–120 costs seven times more than 1–99; you knew this). The blue elite tiers cannot be earned in your own kitchen: they are stages, counted services, the brigade, and finally a menu held at standard for 150 services. Michelin stars go to restaurants, not people — levels 100–120 are the road to the place where stars actually live, built on the Guide's five criteria: product, technique, harmony, personality, consistency. Level 120 is the master cape.

Pace. Cooking most days: Tiers 1–5 ≈ year one, 6–9 ≈ years two and three, 10–12 an endgame measured in years — and 100–120 is a career, not a hobby. Reality checks, not speed limits.

Boss exam rubric — how to grade a boss fight

Score every boss 0–4 in each category. Pass = no zeroes, Safety at 4, average ≥ 3. Recruit an honest taster to score with you — self-graded exams inflate.

ScoreMeaning
0Unsafe, absent, or fundamentally failed
1Works only with heavy help; inconsistent
2Competent in easy conditions; defects remain
3Professional, repeatable, well judged
4Exceptional control; can diagnose and teach it

Categories: safety · organization · product judgment · technique · seasoning & flavor · texture & temperature · timing · presentation · waste & cost · reflection.

Log every serious cook: goal, quantities, temperatures, timeline vs plan, tasting notes, waste, one variable to change next time. The journal is where XP actually accrues; the checkboxes just record it.

Permanent side quests — run alongside every tier
Palate — weekly blind tasting: an ingredient, a seasoning level, a doneness, or a defect.
Journal — record every serious cook; review the month's patterns on the 1st.
Market — visit a grower, fisher, butcher, or producer monthly; buy something you can't name.
Dining — regularly eat food made by cooks better than you and deconstruct it in writing.
Teaching — explain one mastered skill to someone else each month; gaps surface when you teach.
Fitness — sleep, mobility, footwear, stress. Tier 11+ is physical; train for it like one.
Ethics — track waste, sourcing claims, and labor realities behind what you buy.
